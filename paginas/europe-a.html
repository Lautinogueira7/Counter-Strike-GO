Two Regional Major Ranking events for the biggest region

Europe and the CIS region have been combined into one mega region ahead of the Major for the first time, with many of the strongest teams in the world divided into two separate 16-team Regional Major Ranking tournaments to make the qualifying process more manageable. All of the top 15 teams at the time of the qualifiers ending, with exception of [t,furia] who are playing the Americas RMR, will be fighting in the two European RMRs, where fierce underdogs will be out to make a name for themselves against the favorites.

Such a competitive arena will no doubt be a minefield, but the four massive open qualifiers through which 18 of the 32 teams punched their tickets to the European RMRs were already a heartbreaking affair that left behind some strong sides and known names, including Wisla Krakow, SKADE, Apeks, K23, ECSTATIC and Movistar Riders.

Natus Vincere, G2, FaZe, Vitality, Heroic, NIP and Astralis are just some of the world class teams that will be contending for the 16 spots at the Major that have been awarded to the two Europe RMRs, and stars like s1mple, ZywOo and NiKo, the top three players of 2021, will all be competing at the events in Bucharest, Romania, with s1mple and ZwyOo even having a possibility of playing each other in Europe RMR A, while NiKo’s G2 will be in the RMR B among the likes of Gambit and Entropiq in the stacked lineup.

The first of the two Europe RMRs is set to take place at the PGL Studio in Bucharest, Romania, on April 17-20, and will be immediately followed by the second on April 21-24, both featuring a 16-team Swiss stage with best-of-three elimination and advancement matches and a Buchholz seeding system implemented in rounds 3-5. The top four in RMR A will go to the Major in Antwerp, Belgium, as Legends, with the 5-6th placed teams going as Challengers and squads finishing in 7-8th place traveling as Contenders. In RMR B the slot distribution is slightly different, with three Legends, four Challengers and a Contender.
