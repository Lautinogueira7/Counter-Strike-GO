Can the duopoly be disrupted?

Fierce competition may not always be expected in the often overlooked regions of Asia and Oceania, where two Major spots are up for grabs. Cynics, or perhaps realists, will say that TYLOO and Renegades might as well be handed direct invites considering their dominance in Major qualifying tournaments since 2017, but that won't stop the two challengers, Lynn Vision and IHC (formerly Checkmate), from trying to wreak havoc on the region's hegemonic powers. Lynn Vision in particular, who made their way to the Asia RMR through the Middle Eastern qualifier and have been in Europe perfecting their game since late 2021, will be eager to prove that their time abroad has paid off.

The smallest of all of the RMRs, the Asia RMR will only feature the four aforementioned teams with the top two qualifying for the Major in Antwerp, which they will do as Contenders, and the other two leaving empty handed. To earn their spots, the two Major bound squads will have to finish as the top sides in a double-elimination bracket that will take place on April 15-16 at the PGL Studio in Bucharest.
