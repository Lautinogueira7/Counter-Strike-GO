North and South America clash again

Keeping in line with Minors of the past, North and South American teams have been mixed together for the upcoming Americas RMR, although with many South American teams also participating in the North American open qualifiers, a majority of teams at the RMR will thus be from the meridional part of the continent. The South has taken 11 of the 16 slots in play with teams including 00Nation, Imperial, MIBR and paiN. Nine of those teams have at least a Brazilian core if not a full lineup, while Leviatan and 9z also field players from three Spanish speaking nations: Argentina, Uruguay and Chile. The only team to secure a direct invite to the Americas RMR due to their performance at the previous PGL Major in Stockholm, FURIA, will be in contention with many known faces from their time playing top-flight Counter-Strike around the world, including the big three of North America: Liquid, Complexity and Evil Geniuses — although the latter showed they are not yet at full power as they had to go through the second open qualifier after losing in the round of 32 in their first try. One of the big disappointments of the Major cycle in the Americas region so far is GODSENT, who had shown potential in late 2021 by taking big scalps in North America and playing at the PGL Major in Stockholm, but they will miss out this time around as they were knocked out of both North American RMR open qualifiers early. The Americas RMR, which will be played in Bucharest on April 11-14, will grant six spots at the Major, with the winner taking the only Legends spot while silver and bronze will get Challengers status and the three following will fly to Antwerp as Contenders.
